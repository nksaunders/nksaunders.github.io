About Nicholas

I'm currently an undergraduate at UW in Seattle, earning a B.S. in Astronomy and Comprehensive Physics, and a B.A. in Comparative Literature (Cinema Studies).

Stellar Simulations

As the Kepler Space Telescope runs out of fuel, its motion will increase in magnitude and become less predictable. I created stellar simulations to understand how increased motion affects noise in K2 light curves.

Aperture Fitting

In collaboration with Dr. Rodrigo Luger and his EVEREST (EPIC Variability Extraction and Removal for Exoplanet Science Targets) pipeline, I have developed methods to remove instrumental noise from K2 light curves. One method currently in progress is defining apertures around targets to minimize contamination by bright neighbors.
